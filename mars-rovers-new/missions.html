Mission History

Key launches, landings, and discoveries from Mars rover missions.

Chronology

Key Mission Events

June 2003 — Spirit launches from Earth aboard a Delta II rocket.

January 2004 — Spirit lands in Gusev Crater. Opportunity lands later that month in Meridiani Planum.

March 2004 — Opportunity finds strong evidence that water once existed on Mars, detecting hematite spheres in rocks.

2010 — Spirit mission ends after the rover became embedded in soft Martian soil and contact was lost.

November 2011 — Curiosity launches aboard an Atlas V rocket.

August 2012 — Curiosity lands in Gale Crater using the first-ever sky crane landing system.

2013 — Curiosity confirms Mars once had chemical and environmental conditions capable of supporting microbial life.

2019 — Opportunity mission ends after a major dust storm silences the rover. It had traveled over 45 km.

July 2020 — Perseverance launches alongside the Ingenuity helicopter.

February 2021 — Perseverance lands in Jezero Crater, an ancient lakebed.

April 2021 — Ingenuity helicopter makes the first powered, controlled flight on another planet.

Spirit & Opportunity

These twin rovers were sent to study rocks and soil on Mars. Designed for a 90-day mission, both shattered expectations — Opportunity lasted over 14 years.

Mission Details

Both rovers launched in 2003 and landed in 2004 at separate locations. Spirit explored the Columbia Hills inside Gusev Crater while Opportunity investigated Meridiani Planum.

Discoveries

  • Found clear mineralogical evidence that liquid water once flowed on Mars.
  • Detected hematite concretions (nicknamed "blueberries") formed in wet conditions.
  • Opportunity set an off-Earth driving record of 45.16 km before its mission ended.

Curiosity

Curiosity is a car-sized rover designed to study Mars' climate, geology, and habitability. It remains active after more than a decade on the surface.

Mission Details

Launched in 2011 and landed in Gale Crater in 2012, Curiosity climbs the layered slopes of Mount Sharp, reading Mars' geological history like chapters in a book.

Discoveries

  • Confirmed Mars once had environmental conditions suitable for supporting microbial life.
  • Detected complex organic molecules preserved in ancient lake sediments.
  • Measured radiation levels that inform the design of future crewed missions.

Perseverance

NASA's most advanced rover, Perseverance is on a dual mission: searching for signs of ancient life and building a cache of samples for an eventual return to Earth.

Mission Details

Launched in 2020 and landed in Jezero Crater in 2021, Perseverance explores what was once a river delta flowing into an ancient lake — one of the best places to look for preserved biosignatures.

Discoveries

  • Collected the first rock core samples intended for return to Earth.
  • Produced oxygen from the Martian atmosphere via the MOXIE experiment.
  • Documented the Ingenuity helicopter's historic first flights on another world.

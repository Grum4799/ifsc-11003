Buffalo National River

About Buffalo National River

The Buffalo National River in northern Arkansas is one of the few remaining undammed rivers in the lower 48 states. Established as a national river in 1972, it stretches 135 miles through the Ozark Mountains.

Natural Features

Towering bluffs, lush forests, and crystal-clear springs define the character of this remarkable waterway. The river carves through limestone and dolomite formations, creating dramatic canyon landscapes.

Wildlife and Ecology

The river corridor supports white-tailed deer, black bears, wild turkeys, and numerous species of migratory birds. The cold, clear waters are home to smallmouth bass, catfish, and rare freshwater mussels.

Conservation Status

Managed by the National Park Service, the Buffalo National River remains protected from commercial development.

Visitor Information

Ordered List - Peak Visit Seasons

  1. Spring (March - May) - ideal water levels for float trips
  2. Fall (September - November) - stunning foliage and mild temperatures
  3. Summer (June - August) - swimming, fishing, and camping
  4. Winter (December - February) - solitude and wildlife watching

Unordered List - Popular Activities

  • Canoe and kayak float trips
  • Hiking more than 100 miles of maintained trails
  • Rock climbing on limestone bluffs
  • Wildlife photography and bird watching

Definition List - Key Terms

National River
A designation protecting a river corridor from development while allowing compatible recreational use.
Float Trip
A multi-day paddling excursion traveling downstream, camping along the riverbanks each night.
Karst Topography
A landscape formed from dissolved limestone, characterized by caves, sinkholes, and springs.

Notable Quotes

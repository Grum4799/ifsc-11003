Meet the Rovers

NASA has sent several rovers to Mars to study its surface, climate, and history.

2003 – 2019

Spirit & Opportunity

Twin rovers designed to study Martian rocks and soil. Both lasted far longer than expected and found strong evidence that water once existed on Mars.

View Mission

2011 – Present

Curiosity

A car-sized rover studying Mars’ climate and geology. It confirmed that Mars once had conditions that could support life.

View Mission

2020 – Present

Perseverance

NASA’s newest rover, searching for signs of ancient life and collecting samples that may be returned to Earth.

View Mission

The Mission

Why Explore Mars?

Follow the Water

Mars once had liquid water. Studying it helps scientists understand how planets change over time.

Search for Life

Rovers look for signs that simple life may have existed on Mars in the past.

Prepare for Humans

Rovers test technology and gather information that could help future human missions.

Study the Solar System

Mars helps scientists compare planets and learn more about how Earth and others formed.

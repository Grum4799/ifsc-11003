Mission History

Key launches, landings, and discoveries from Mars rover missions.

Key Mission Events

June 2003 — Spirit launches from Earth.

January 2004 — Spirit lands in Gusev Crater. Opportunity lands later that month.

March 2004 — Opportunity finds strong evidence that water once existed on Mars.

2010 — Spirit mission ends after getting stuck in soft soil.

November 2011 — Curiosity launches.

August 2012 — Curiosity lands in Gale Crater using a sky crane system.

2013 — Curiosity confirms Mars once had conditions that could support life.

2019 — Opportunity mission ends after a major dust storm.

July 2020 — Perseverance launches.

February 2021 — Perseverance lands in Jezero Crater.

April 2021 — Ingenuity helicopter makes the first flight on another planet.

Spirit & Opportunity

These twin rovers were sent to study rocks and soil on Mars. They were designed to last 90 days but operated for years.

Mission Details

Both rovers launched in 2003 and landed in 2004. They explored different parts of Mars and collected data about the planet’s surface.

Discoveries

  • Found clear evidence that water once existed on Mars.
  • Studied rocks and soil to understand Mars’ history.
  • Opportunity set a distance record for off-Earth travel.

Curiosity

Curiosity is a larger rover designed to study Mars’ climate and geology.

Mission Details

Launched in 2011 and landed in 2012, Curiosity explores Gale Crater and studies rock layers.

Discoveries

  • Confirmed Mars once had conditions suitable for life.
  • Found organic molecules in rocks.
  • Studied radiation levels for future human missions.

Perseverance

Perseverance is NASA’s newest rover, focused on finding signs of ancient life.

Mission Details

Launched in 2020 and landed in 2021, it explores Jezero Crater, an ancient lakebed.

Discoveries

  • Collected rock samples for future return to Earth.
  • Tested oxygen production from the Martian atmosphere.
  • Worked with the Ingenuity helicopter.
